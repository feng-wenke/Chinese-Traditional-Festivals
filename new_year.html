Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year, also known as Spring Festival, is probably the most important traditional festival for Chinese people. It is the first day of a Chinese lunar year. It usually falls in January or early February on the Gregorian calendar.

On Chinese New Year's Eve, the Chinese would have a dinner with family members, where people would enjoy traditional foods like dumplings and spring rolls. People come from differnt regions in China might prefer differnt kinds of traditional foods.

Customs include making traditional foods together, writing antithetical couplets and setting off firecrackers. A famous poet Wang, Anshi from Song Dynasty once wrote "Amid the boom of firecrackers a year has come to an end (爆竹声中一岁除)".
