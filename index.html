Chinese Traditional Festivals - Overview

Chinese people celebrate quite a few traditional festivals based on the Chinese lunar calendar (农历) every year. These events are formed during a long history. Some of them could be traced back to Qin Dynasty (221-206 BC).

As these festivals are important in Chinese culture, Chinese people still follow some of the conventions nowadays. During these festivals, Chinese people usaully meet with family members and friends, and eat traditional foods corresponding to each festival. For example, on Mid-Autumn Festival, people will eat mooncakes.

Below is an overview of the most popular Chinese traditional festivals, including Chinese New Year, Lantern Festival, Qing Ming Festival, Dragon-Boat Festival, Double Seventh Festival, Zhongyuan Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, Double Ninth Festival, and Dongzhi Festival.
